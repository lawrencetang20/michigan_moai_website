Healing from Hate Project

Victims of hate crimes often experience lasting trauma, including depression, anxiety, and insomnia, and frequently seek therapy to cope. However, language barriers and lack of cultural competency among therapists make accessing effective mental health care difficult, especially for non-English speakers. To address this, ACJ plans to partner with nonprofits like Michigan Moai to connect victims—particularly in the AANHPI community—with licensed, culturally competent therapists who speak their native language. By leveraging PSYPACT (which allows cross-state telehealth), ACJ will build a referral network, fund initial therapy sessions through grants (since insurance often often won’t cover out-of-state care), and run an awareness campaign. This initiative will improve access to quality, timely mental health care for hate crime victims in the Detroit area and potentially across Michigan.
