Projects & Workshops

Beyond the directory.

Workshops, partnerships, and community programs that build the kind of long-term care infrastructure a single directory can't — focused on AANHPI youth, summer camps, and mental health education in Michigan.

Community partner

Association of Chinese Americans (ACA)

We partner with ACA Detroit on youth programming, mental health workshops, and summer camp training across Metro Detroit.

Visit ACA Detroit
Fall 2025 Workshop · Students

International Student Services Workshop

In Fall 2025, we hosted a workshop for University of Michigan international students — a panel and peer-led conversation on the unique challenges they face in today's climate, plus stress-management strategies, community-building resources, and ways to support one another through shared experience.

July 2025 Workshop · Youth

Anti-Bullying Workshop

An interactive anti-bullying workshop for the Asian American youth summer camp hosted by ACA Detroit. The session focused on empowering campers to recognize, respond to, and prevent bullying — particularly forms rooted in racism, xenophobia, and cultural stigma. Through group discussions, role-playing, and culturally relevant scenarios, participants built skills in assertive communication, allyship, and community care.

Dec 2024 Workshop · High school

Bubble Tea Mental Health Workshop

A hands-on workshop in partnership with American Citizens for Justice and the Michigan Asian Pacific American Association, inviting high school students to engage with mental health awareness through a welcoming, interactive experience. Participants created their own boba drinks, learned about peer mental health advocacy, and were introduced to leadership opportunities on a youth mental health advisory committee.

June 2024 Training · Counselors

Chinese American Summer Camp Counselor Training

A culturally responsive counselor training for a Chinese American youth summer camp. The training equipped camp counselors with tools to support campers' mental health, address identity-related challenges, and foster affirming spaces — with emphasis on cultural humility, communication strategy, and the unique experiences of Chinese American youth in intergenerational and bicultural contexts.
